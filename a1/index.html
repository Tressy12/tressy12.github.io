About the Club

Manchester City Football Club, also known as Man City, play in the English Premier League which is the top division of English football. Man City was originally founded in 1880 under the name St. Mark’s. The name was changed in 1894 and has remained ever since. Manchester City is owned by Abu Dhabi's Mansour bin Zayed Al Nahyan and is managed by Pep Guardiola. Manchester City's top scorer of all time is Sergio Agüero with 260 goals in 389 appearances. Manchester City also competes in other competitions such as the Champions League, the FA Cup, and the Carabao Cup. City have seen a rise to the top of the league after their famous 3-2 win against QPR that landed them their first league win in 44 years. City’s traditional colours are sky blue and white and are fierce rivals with Manchester United.

Our History

Manchester City Football Club, also known as MCFC, is a professional football club located in Manchester, England. It was founded in 1880 as St. Mark's (West Gorton) and later changed its name to Ardwick Association Football Club in 1887, and finally to Manchester City Football Club in 1894. The club has a storied history, with successes including English Premier League titles, FA Cup titles, League Cup titles, and European competition victories. Manchester City has experienced ups and downs throughout its history. In the 1960s, the club achieved success with league titles, FA Cup wins, and a European Cup Winners' Cup victory. However, the club faced challenges in the following decades and was relegated to the third tier of English football in 1998. In 2008, Manchester City was bought by Abu Dhabi United Group, leading to a revival for the club. Since then, MCFC has become a major force in English football, winning multiple Premier League titles and reaching the final of the UEFA Champions League in 2021. The club is known for its passionate fan base and attractive style of play, with notable players representing Manchester City over the years. The team plays its home matches at the Etihad Stadium, and the club has been involved in various community and charitable initiatives. Manchester City aims to maintain its position as a top football club, continuing its pursuit of success on and off the field.
