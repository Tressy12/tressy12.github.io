Our History

Los Pollos Hermanos was founded by Chilean nationals and business partners Gus Fring and Max Arciniega in Mexico. Los Pollos Hermanos was inspired by recipes cooked by Gus Frings’s uncles. Eventually, Gus emigrated to the United States and opened branches primarily in New Mexico, and the chain grew to have 14 locations throughout the American Southwest. Los Pollos Hermanos trucks were used to smuggle drugs manufactured by the show's fictional drug cartel across the Mexico–United States border. A large chicken farm for Los Pollos Hermanos at the outskirts of Albuquerque served as a front where the incoming meth were processed and shipped to the various stores and other destinations. Fring kept the restaurants themselves clean of any business related to drugs.

The company eventually became a subsidiary of German shipping conglomerate, Madrigal Electromotive GmbH, whose connections Fring used to further increase the outreach of both his legal and illegal operations. After Fring's death at the hands of Walter White and Hector Salamanca, the chain was closed, with the flagship restaurant in Albuquerque being replaced with a Twisters.
